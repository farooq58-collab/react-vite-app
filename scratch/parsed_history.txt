I have refined and simplified the toggling mechanism to prevent the overlay from getting stuck or double-triggering.

### What Was Fixed:
1. **Removed Breakpoint Locks:** Previously, the click-toggle event was restricted to `window.innerWidth < 1024` pixels. Tapping on screen sizes around or above `1024px` (like iPads, tablets, or simulated dev environments) triggered simulated CSS hovers that got stuck without executing the click-toggle state. Removing this restriction enables uniform toggle support on touch devices.
2. **Fixed Dynamic Hit-Testing (`pointer-events` Bug):** Toggling `pointer-events` dynamically between `pointer-events-none` (default hidden) and `pointer-events-auto` (visible) during active touch events confuses browser hit-testing engines on touch devices. This was causing taps to get "swallowed" or double-dispatched (toggling ON and immediately OFF, resulting in "no effect appearing"). 
   - We updated the overlay container to **always** utilize `pointer-events-auto`. This stabilizes the click targets and makes sure every tap behaves as a clean toggle, whether opening or closing the overlay.
